AI Isn't Reducing Workloads—It's Straining Employees
Research Top Pick Fortune · March 13, 2026

An ActivTrak analysis of 10,584 workers before and after AI adoption found that time spent emailing doubled and deep focus work sessions fell by 9%. Workers are spending more cognitive energy coordinating with and reviewing AI outputs rather than doing their core work. The data directly contradicts the assumption that AI tools naturally shift employee time toward higher-value tasks.

Why it matters: This is the counternarrative every founder pitching "AI will free up my team" needs to hear—useful ammunition for Krissi's advising conversations on realistic AI adoption planning and avoiding productivity theater.

Gartner: The Top Four Talent Acquisition Trends for 2026
Research Gartner · October 2025

Gartner's TA research identifies four trends reshaping recruiting: AI-first hiring for high-volume frontline roles, recruiter role evolution toward strategic advisory, a mandatory rethink of early-career pipelines as entry-level jobs evaporate, and AI proficiency assessment entering the hiring process. By 2027, Gartner predicts 75% of hiring processes will include formal AI skills certification.

Why it matters: The recruiter-as-strategic-advisor shift is directly relevant for Krissi's clients deciding whether to invest in upskilling their existing TA team or rebuild from scratch—and the entry-level pipeline collapse is material for any startup scaling fast.

Thought Leadership
Why Gen AI Feels So Threatening to Workers
Thought Leadership Harvard Business Review · March 2026

HBR examines the psychological roots of AI workplace anxiety, distinguishing three distinct threat types: threat to identity (AI undermines what makes me uniquely valuable), threat to job security (AI will replace me), and threat to mastery (AI makes my expertise feel irrelevant). Workers experiencing identity threat show higher disengagement than those worried purely about job loss—and are harder to retain even when their roles are safe.

Why it matters: Founders need this framework when onboarding new hires into AI-forward teams—understanding which threat type an employee is experiencing determines how to communicate and manage the transition effectively.

AI & Recruiting Tools
AI Talent vs. AI Tools: What Early-Stage Startups Should Actually Hire For in 2026
Recruiting Bristow Holland · 2026

A practical decision framework for early-stage founders: hire humans where their judgment has outsized business impact—AI engineers who refine models, data engineers who ensure data quality, and forward-deployed engineers who adapt AI tools to customer problems. Roles where off-the-shelf AI already performs adequately (e.g., basic content, templated customer support) shouldn't be staffed with expensive human talent in 2026.

Why it matters: This maps directly to the conversations Krissi has with seed and Series A founders deciding their first five hires—gives her a shareable, concrete framework rather than general guidance.

When Using AI Leads to "Brain Fry"
Research Seen before Harvard Business Review · March 5, 2026 First surfaced March 11

A BCG-backed HBR study defines "brain fry" as cognitive fatigue from constant AI tool oversight—affecting about 1 in 7 workers. High performers managing multiple AI systems report more errors and a ~10% higher intent to quit. Workers who use AI only to automate routine tasks (vs. those who use it to expand task volume) actually see lower burnout scores.

Why it's still here: Today's Fortune workload data validates this from a different angle—together these two pieces form the most compelling current evidence that AI is creating a new kind of burnout that startup leaders aren't designing for yet.

Management as AI Superpower
Thought Leadership Seen before One Useful Thing (Ethan Mollick) · January 27, 2026 First surfaced March 11

Mollick's Wharton MBA class built complete startups in four days using AI agents. The highest performers weren't the strongest coders—they were the students who could clearly define goals, delegate precisely, and quality-check outputs at speed. The insight: traditional management skills—clarity, accountability, feedback—are the competitive differentiator in agentic AI workflows.

Why it's still here: Remains the single most quotable piece for Krissi's speaking work on what leadership looks like when AI does the execution—especially powerful paired with today's Gartner data on recruiter role evolution.
