Thought Leadership
GPT-5: It Just Does Stuff
Thought Leadership Top Pick One Useful Thing (Ethan Mollick) · March 2026

Mollick's first-person account of what GPT-5 actually feels like to use: it doesn't wait to be asked. Prompted to generate startup ideas, it returned unprompted landing pages, LinkedIn copy, and a financial model — deliverables that "would have taken a team of MBAs a couple hours to work through." The model has shifted from executing instructions to anticipating needs, and Mollick argues the bottleneck is now knowing what to ask for, not how to produce it.

Why it matters: This is Krissi's best current evidence that AI has crossed a threshold — from assistant to autonomous collaborator — making it essential framing for any talk on what "working with AI" will mean for the next generation of hires.

New Job Families & Org Design
Why LinkedIn Is Replacing PMs with AI-Powered "Full-Stack Builders"
New Roles Lenny's Newsletter / Tomer Cohen (LinkedIn CPO) · December 2025

LinkedIn CPO Tomer Cohen scrapped the company's Associate Product Manager program and replaced it with an Associate Product Builder track — training new hires in coding, design, and PM skills simultaneously. Cohen introduced a formal "Full Stack Builder" title and career ladder, with small cross-functional pods replacing traditional functional silos. His explicit goal: automate everything except vision, empathy, creativity, and high-quality judgment in ambiguous situations.

Why it matters: This is the most concrete real-world case study of a major tech company redesigning its entire early-career talent model for the AI era — directly usable when advising AI startup founders on org structure, job title strategy, and what to recruit for in 2026.

GPT-5 and the New Era of Work
OpenAI OpenAI · March 2026

OpenAI's official framing of how GPT-5.4 — released March 5 — changes professional work. The model matches or exceeds industry professionals in 83% of knowledge work comparisons (up from 71% for GPT-5.2), supports 1M token context, and is the first general-purpose model with native computer-use capabilities. Enterprise users report saving 40–60 minutes daily on average; heavy users claim 10+ hours a week.

Why it matters: This is the authoritative baseline for what Krissi's clients are actually buying into when they say "we're adopting AI" — critical for calibrating expectations when advising founders on realistic productivity timelines and what roles to hire vs. automate.

Research: How AI Is Changing the Labor Market
Research Seen before Harvard Business Review · March 2026 First surfaced March 12

An 8-month Berkeley/HBR study tracking AI adoption across 40+ companies found that generative AI is reshaping white-collar work rather than uniformly eliminating it — but the impact is uneven, with entry-level and customer-service roles most disrupted. The study provides occupational-level data on which tasks are being automated first and which human capabilities are becoming more valuable as a result.

Why it's still here: Today's OpenAI productivity data and Mollick's capability piece both reinforce the same conclusion — this HBR study provides the rigorous academic foundation that makes those narratives credible in a talk or client conversation.
